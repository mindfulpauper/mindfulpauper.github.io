Meat This
5 February 2015

We'd like to share one of our favourite quick dinners. It tastes so meaty you won't want to serve it to vegetarians.

Take 200g of brown lentils and cook until tender but retaining some bite. (We do this in our pressure cooker, aptly named Big-boy daddy and it takes 9 mins)

Once they're done, put the heat on low and push the lentils to the sides of the pan and melt some butter or oil in the centre. Add a generous teaspoon of freshly ground cumin and 2 big cloves of minced garlic when the butter starts to foam or when the oil gets hot. Fry these for a couple of minutes before stirring the lentils back in.

Add half a can of plum tomatoes into the pan, withholding as much of the liquid as possible, along with a small tea cup of water. Go crazy and mash the tomatoes up. Cook for a further 5-10 mins until everything is tender. Add 2 dinner spoons of tahini and 1 dinner spoon of red wine vinegar or lemon. We like smashing the lentils up a bit to further thicken. Season to taste and tuck in.

This is not a pretty dish however it tastes exquisite with slices of red onions and a drizzle of rapeseed oil.

Eat with hands, spoons or bread.
