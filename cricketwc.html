Cricket World Cup
23 February 2015

With the 2015 Cricket World Cup having started last weekend, we thought we'd take a look at some of the stories and issues that we feel are important in some of the countries taking part.
Initially we'd considered doing a guide to the tournament explaining a reason to support each team and reasons not to, but when you analyse professional sporting events, you realise it is not an innocent celebration of sport, but an extension of the corporate world. A look at the principal sponsors of the tournament give you an idea of what is going on [1]:

Reliance (Indian conglomerate - Communication, Power, Rail, Entertainment)
Hyundai (South Korean conglomerate - Cars, ships, construction, defence)
LG (Electronics)
Pepsi (Drinks/Snacks)
Emirates (Airline)
SAP (Software)
ANZ (Bank)
Moneygram (Money transfer)
MRF Tyres (Tyres)
Castrol (Engine Oil) - Owned by BP
Hublot (Watches) - Owned by LVMH
Reebok (Sportswear) - Owned by Adidas
Tui (Beer) - Owned by Heineken Asia Pacific
VB - Victoria Bitter (Beer) - Owned by SABMiller
Wolf Blass (Wine) - Owned by Treasury Wine Estates
Matua (Wine) - Owned by Treasury Wine Estates
Star Sports (TV) - Owned by Fox/News Corporation

Over the coming days, we will be posting articles focusing on a number of the countries participating in the World Cup specifically looking at food security and related issues.
